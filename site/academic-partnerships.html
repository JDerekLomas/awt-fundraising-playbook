Academic Partnership Opportunities

Premier institutions for Renaissance, medieval, and esoteric studies collaboration

Partnership Strategy Overview

These institutions represent the world's leading centers for Renaissance, medieval, and esoteric studies. They can provide:

  • Scholarly validation - Academic advisory boards and peer review
  • Co-publishing opportunities - Joint digital editions and scholarly commentary
  • Graduate student workforce - Translation assistance and metadata creation
  • Funding connections - Access to NEH, AHRC, ERC grants and institutional partnerships
  • Network effects - Introductions to donors, foundations, and other scholars

1. Warburg Institute

HIGH PRIORITY

Overview

Location: University of London, UK

Focus: The premier institution for the study of cultural continuity, Renaissance philosophy, and the classical tradition. Founded by Aby Warburg, specializes in the transmission of ideas from antiquity through the Renaissance.

Key Contacts

Director

Prof. Bill Sherman

Email: warburg@sas.ac.uk (general inquiries)

Research interests: History of reading, Renaissance book culture

Key Faculty

Prof. Charles Burnett - Medieval Arabic and Latin translation traditions

Dr. Raphaële Mouren - Head of Library, rare books specialist

Prof. Guido Giglioni - Renaissance natural philosophy, alchemy

Partnership Opportunities

  • Academic Advisory Board - Warburg scholars reviewing translations
  • Joint Digital Editions - Co-publishing critical editions with scholarly apparatus
  • Symposia - Host joint conferences on Hermetic texts and Renaissance transmission
  • Library Collaboration - Cross-institutional catalog sharing
  • PhD Student Engagement - Dissertations using AWT digitized texts

Funding Programs

Warburg Research Fellowships - Could support scholars working on AWT materials

AHRC Partnership Grants - Warburg can be lead applicant for UK Arts & Humanities Research Council grants (up to £1M+)

Outreach Strategy

Start with library contact (Dr. Mouren), emphasize the BPH collection's alignment with Warburg's mission. Offer exclusive early access to digitized texts. Request introduction to Director Sherman for formal partnership discussion.

90-Day Action Plan for Academic Partnerships

Month 1: Foundation Building

Week 1-2: Email outreach to Amsterdam partners (UvA, Peter Forshaw)
  • Request in-person meeting at Embassy of the Free Mind
  • Offer tour of BPH collection
  • Discuss MA student engagement
Week 2-3: Reach out to UK partners (Warburg, Exeter)
  • Email library contact at Warburg (Dr. Mouren)
  • Email Programme Director at Exeter (Dr. Chaitow)
  • Propose virtual presentations to their communities
Week 3-4: US outreach batch 1 (high-value scholars)
  • Brian Copenhaver (UCLA) - Hermetic expertise
  • Anthony Grafton (Princeton) - prestige endorsement
  • William Newman (Indiana) - alchemy expertise

Month 2: Relationship Development

Week 5-6: Follow-up meetings & virtual presentations
  • Schedule Zoom presentations for interested programs
  • Create "Scholar's Guide to BPH Collection" PDF
  • Offer sample digitized texts as "teasers"
Week 6-7: US institutional outreach
  • Contact I Tatti library (Michael Rocke)
  • Contact Folger digital initiatives
  • Contact Huntington research director
Week 7-8: Propose specific pilot projects
  • UvA: 10 MA students create critical editions
  • Exeter: Summer translation workshop
  • Warburg: Joint symposium on Hermetic texts

Month 3: Formalization & Grant Planning

Week 9-10: Draft MOUs & partnership agreements
  • Formalize relationships with 2-3 key partners
  • Establish advisory board (5-7 scholars)
  • Create partner benefits document
Week 10-11: Grant application planning
  • Identify NEH and AHRC deadlines
  • Find 1-2 partners willing to be lead applicants
  • Draft letters of support from other partners
Week 11-12: Launch pilot projects
  • Begin student engagement programs
  • Schedule first joint event (symposium or webinar)
  • Announce partnerships publicly for credibility

Sample Outreach Email to Scholars

Subject: Partnership opportunity: Digitizing 5,000 Renaissance texts from Amsterdam's Bibliotheca Philosophica Hermetica

Dear Professor [Name],

I'm reaching out from the Ancient Wisdom Trust regarding an exciting digitization project that I believe would be of direct interest to your research.

We're working to digitize and translate 5,000 rare Renaissance texts from the Bibliotheca Philosophica Hermetica in Amsterdam—one of the world's premier collections of Hermetic, alchemical, and esoteric materials. The BPH collection includes [mention 2-3 specific texts relevant to their work].

Currently, 95% of Renaissance texts lack modern translations, and many scholars only know these works through citations. We're using AI-assisted translation (reviewed by classical scholars) to change that, making these foundational texts freely accessible to researchers worldwide.

We'd love to explore partnership opportunities:

  • Early access to digitized texts for your research
  • Academic advisory role to guide text selection and translation priorities
  • Opportunities for graduate students to work with primary sources
  • [If applicable: Joint grant applications for NEH/AHRC funding]

Would you be available for a 20-minute call to discuss how this project might support your work? I'd be happy to provide sample digitized texts and more details about our approach.

The project website is at [URL], and you can learn more about the BPH collection at embassyofthefreemind.com.

Thank you for considering this opportunity. I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Ancient Wisdom Trust
