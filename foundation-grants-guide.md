# Ancient Wisdom Trust - Foundation & Grants Guide

## Overview

This guide outlines strategies for securing foundation grants to support the digitization and translation of 5,000 NeoLatin Renaissance texts.

**Target:** $200,000-$300,000 from foundation sources (40-60% of $500K goal)

---

## I. Grant Readiness Checklist

Before approaching foundations, ensure you have:

- [ ] **501(c)(3) status** or fiscal sponsor
- [ ] **Mission statement** (clear, compelling, concise)
- [ ] **Board of Directors** with relevant expertise
- [ ] **Audited financials** (or reviewed if budget under $500K)
- [ ] **Annual report** (even a simple one)
- [ ] **Project budget** (detailed, realistic)
- [ ] **Logic model or theory of change**
- [ ] **Evaluation plan** (how you'll measure success)
- [ ] **Letters of support** from partner institutions
- [ ] **Key staff bios** and CVs

## III. Application Process

### Step 1: Research & Qualify (2-4 weeks)
- Review foundation website, guidelines, past grants
- Check deadline calendar
- Confirm eligibility
- Note application requirements
- Identify program officer if possible

### Step 2: Cultivate (1-3 months)
- Send Letter of Inquiry (LOI) if required
- Request informational call with program officer
- Attend foundation events if available
- Connect through board/network introductions
- Follow foundation on social media

### Step 3: Apply (2-4 weeks)
- Follow all guidelines exactly
- Answer questions asked (not what you wish they asked)
- Include required attachments
- Proofread carefully
- Submit before deadline (never on the deadline)

### Step 4: Follow Up (1-6 months)
- Send brief acknowledgment email
- Respond promptly to any requests
- Provide updates if timeline is long
- Thank regardless of outcome
- Ask for feedback if declined

### Step 5: Steward (Ongoing if funded)
- Acknowledge publicly (if permitted)
- Submit reports on time
- Invite to events
- Share success stories
- Lay groundwork for renewal

## VI. Common Application Components

### Project Narrative
- **Problem statement:** What need are you addressing?
- **Goals and objectives:** What will you accomplish?
- **Methods:** How will you do it?
- **Timeline:** When will activities occur?
- **Evaluation:** How will you measure success?
- **Sustainability:** What happens after the grant?

### Budget
- **Personnel:** Staff time, contractors, consultants
- **Equipment:** Digitization, technology
- **Supplies:** Materials, software
- **Travel:** Site visits, conferences
- **Other:** Subgrants, indirect costs

### Attachments Commonly Requested
- IRS determination letter
- Board list with affiliations
- Current year budget
- Most recent financials
- Staff bios
- Letters of support
- Organizational chart

## IX. Grant Writing Tips

### Do:
- Start with the funder's priorities, not yours
- Use clear, jargon-free language
- Quantify impact where possible
- Show, don't tell (include examples)
- Demonstrate organizational capacity
- Connect to broader significance

### Don't:
- Submit generic proposals
- Ignore page limits or formatting requirements
- Overstate capacity or understate challenges
- Request funding for general operations (unless invited)
- Forget to proofread
- Wait until the last minute

### Winning Phrases (Adapt, don't copy)
- "For the first time in 500 years..."
- "The only collection of its kind in the world..."
- "UNESCO-recognized significance..."
- "Freely accessible to scholars and the public..."
- "At the intersection of cultural preservation and artificial intelligence..."
